## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Does this look AI-generated? No.** This reads as hand-built product UI, and mostly good hand-built product UI. The tab page is the high point — flat cards, Fraunces title + Hanken everywhere else, tabular-nums on all figures, warm sage restrained to chevron/empty-icon, dark mode genuinely first-class. No gradient text, no glassmorphism, no side-stripes, no hero-metric template, no eyebrow kickers, no numbered section markers, no `repeating-linear-gradient`, no sketchy SVG, no meta-criticism copy. Cards are flat at rest (border XOR shadow honored); radii are 20/12/8, never the codex 32px+ over-round.

**The strangeness is specific and accidental, not template-driven, and concentrated entirely on the detail page:** three add-button vocabularies, three focus-ring styles, two conflicting CSS definitions for the same class, immediate-destructive actions, and a clay chart color that breaks the brand rule.

**Deterministic scan:** `detect.mjs` on the two Hijos TSX files returned **0 findings (clean)**. The detector found no slop-template violations — every issue below is semantic/UX, caught by the design review, not the pattern-matcher.

## Overall Impression

The Hijos *tab* is a calm, well-judged list — this is the part a Linear/Notion user would not pause at. The Hijos *detail* page is overloaded and carries the surface's real risk: it silently destroys the high-stakes data the app exists to protect. The single biggest opportunity is **trust** — confirm before delete, signal mutation state, and stop lying in copy about voice entry that isn't here.

## What's Working

1. **The tab page is genuinely restrained.** Flat cards, one sage, monogram avatars with per-name warmth (cute-but-adult), tabular ages/metrics, Fraunces-over-Hanken. The Quiet Sage Rule is honored here.
2. **Dark mode is first-class and actually works.** Tokens flip coherently, muted text clears AA on both surfaces, monogram tones re-derive for dark, chart uses token colors. Few apps get this right.
3. **Progressive disclosure instinct is correct where applied** — tallas history collapsed behind a count toggle, visita detail as a swap-in view rather than a modal.

## Priority Issues

### [P0] Destructive actions fire with no confirm and no undo
- **Why it matters:** `deleteMutation.mutate(m.id)` (HijoDetailPage.tsx:404), `deleteVisit.mutate(v.id)` (:604), and talla delete (SizesSection.tsx:107) destroy growth measurements, diagnoses, and sizes irreversibly with a single 32px thumb tap and zero feedback. For an app whose promise is *reducing the mental load of remembering*, silent irreversible loss is the worst possible failure mode — and peak-end means users remember the one time it happened.
- **Fix:** Reuse the existing `.hijo-confirm` inline-confirm pattern (children.css:688) — tap trash → row swaps to "¿Borrar? [Borrar] [Cancelar]" — plus an undo toast on success (the toast system landed in commit d29a6f6).
- **Suggested command:** `$impeccable harden`

### [P1] Silent mutation failures — no pending state, no error handling
- **Why it matters:** `create.mutate(input, { onSuccess: onDone })` has no `onError` (HijoDetailPage.tsx:194-201, 567-571). A failed save leaves the form open with no message; the parent walks away believing the pediatrician's number is recorded. It is not.
- **Fix:** Add `onError` wiring to the toast system; disable submit + show a spinner while `mutation.isPending`.
- **Suggested command:** `$impeccable harden`

### [P1] Detail page is overloaded; flat hierarchy, no single focus
- **Why it matters:** Summary + Tallas + Crecimiento (2 charts + form + table) + Visitas (filter + form + list + detail) all on one scroll. The primary task — "log Leo's height" — is separated from its list by two charts. Cognitive-load checklist fails single-focus, visual-hierarchy, and minimal-choices (a growth row presents 5 parallel affordances, 2 of them destructive).
- **Fix:** Collapse Crecimiento and Visitas into a tabbed/accordion sub-nav (Resumen / Crecimiento / Visitas), each with its primary action at the top; or move charts behind a "Ver gráfica" toggle mirroring tallas history.
- **Suggested command:** `$impeccable distill`

### [P1] Inconsistent component vocabulary on the detail page
- **Why it matters:** Three add-button styles (filled-sage at hijos-tab.css:399 silently overriding the quiet one at :185; glyph "+" at sizes.css:58 vs SVG PlusIcon elsewhere), three focus-ring styles (solid 2px :449, soft color-mix 25% :687, double outline sizes.css:171), and 32px row-action buttons vs the 44px `icon-btn` used on the sibling children surface. A Stripe/Linear user *does* pause at this. The 32px targets also breach WCAG 2.5.5.
- **Fix:** Pick one quiet add-button (the :185 surface-2 version is more on-brand for a per-section action), one focus ring (`--ds-focus-ring` already in children.css:229), and bump row actions to the 44px `icon-btn`. Delete the duplicate definition.
- **Suggested command:** `$impeccable polish`

### [P1] Clay on the Peso chart breaks "clay = needs-you-now"
- **Why it matters:** `<GrowthChart color="var(--ds-attention)" />` (HijoDetailPage.tsx:382) uses the urgency-only hue as a mere second-series color — a direct breach of the brand rule.
- **Fix:** Use a second sage tone (`--ds-primary-hover`) or an ink-muted line for Peso; reserve clay strictly.
- **Suggested command:** `$impeccable colorize`

## Persona Red Flags

**Casey — distracted, one-handed mobile user (mandatory):**
- 32px trash buttons in the thumb zone = accidental, silent deletes (:404, :604).
- Logging a measurement is 6 taps and requires scrolling past Tallas + two charts; the "Medida" add button is divorced from the list it relates to.
- No active-filter chip on visitas — re-finding "what did I filter to" is a working-memory tax.
- **Will fat-finger-delete a visita and never know it's gone.**

**Sam — accessibility user:**
- 32px targets fail WCAG 2.5.5 (44px) — visita-action-btn (:374), growth-row__action (:733).
- The visita row is a `<button>` next to sibling edit/delete `<button>`s in the same `<li>` — a screen reader hears "button, button, button" with no labelled relationship. Needs `role="group"` + `aria-label` on the `<li>`.
- Focus visibility is inconsistent across the page; the soft `color-mix 25%` ring (:687) is near-invisible.

**Tired parent glancing one-handed between tasks (project-specific):**
- Opens the page at 11pm to log a height; must scan past Tallas + Altura chart + Peso chart to find the list, then scroll back up to find "Medida."
- "Dicta una medida por voz" (:368) promises hands-free that isn't on this surface.
- No "last updated" timestamp — "did I already log this month?" is unanswerable at a glance.

## Questions to Consider

1. **What is the Hijos detail page *for*?** It's a dashboard, editor, logbook, and clinic archive simultaneously. If a parent opens it with one task — log a height — why does that task require scrolling past two charts? Should Crecimiento be the focused default?
2. **Why does the children-management surface have a more mature component vocabulary (confirm-on-delete, 44px icon buttons, one focus ring) than the Hijos surface next to it?** The patterns exist — they weren't carried over.
3. **Is "Dicta una medida por voz" a promise you can keep here?** If voice lives on the MCP/on-device path, this empty-state copy is a broken signpost. Bring the affordance here, or fix the copy.
